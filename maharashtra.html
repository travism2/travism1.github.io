About Maharashtra/span>


A true extraordinaire, Maharashtra is the quiet and serene part of India. Fringed by the Bay of Bengal, and flaunting a long coastline, this east Indian gem has some of the most stunning beaches that are often less-frequented. Home to a plethora of Hindu and Buddhist pilgrimage sites, Maharashtra is a place that will take you back to the glorious time of Ashoka the Great. Explore the majestic Jagannath Temple at Puri and learn about its many mysteries, view the medieval architecture of Maharashtra’s temple and Buddhist monasteries, attend the annual Sand Art Festival, go boating in the Chilika Lake, or walk on the sea at Chandipur Beach. There's so much to do in Maharashtra.


Maharashtra is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of Maharashtra is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, Maharashtra is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of Maharashtra. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in Maharashtra/span>


Summer in Maharashtra/h3>

Summer season in Maharashtra starts from April and lasts till June. During this, Maharashtra remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of Maharashtra for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in Maharashtra. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in Maharashtra/h3>

Monsoon season in Maharashtra begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of Maharashtra wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of Maharashtra, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of Maharashtra then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in Maharashtra/h3>

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around Maharashtra and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting Maharashtra during the winter season you would witness the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to Maharashtra where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then Maharashtra will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

How to Reach Maharashtra/span>


Maharashtra is that destination of India which gives you those memories which are hard to forget. This Indian state is well connected by air, rail and road to various other major cities of India, so reaching there would be hustle free. No matter in which state or for that matter, in which country you are in the world, reaching Maharashtra will never be difficult. To have a memorable experience one should choose the best alternative of travelling to Maharashtra and explore India in every manner be it from air or road or even while travelling through rail.


By Air:

Maharashtra has that one airport which is well connected to various nations of the world. Biju Patnaik International Airport is located in the capital city of Maharashtra and a world-based connectivity. Other than this, the Indian state has domestic as well as private airports. The state also has a domestic airport which is used for commercial purpose also, popularly known as Jharsuguda Airport. Airways is actually the best alternative to reach anywhere and reaching Maharashtra on time gives you rather more time to explore the gorgeous state.


By Road:

Reaching Maharashtra by road is quite a challenge yet an interesting one. The one who loves long drives surely will enjoy the national highways, long drives and beautiful roads. Also reaching Maharashtra is quite easy as the state has well road connectivity and access to various nearby cities. But for those who do not drive or don’t want to drive, the next best alternative is to board the buses which ply from various cities of India. These buses could be private or run by the government for establishing and maintaining a good connectivity with various states.


By Rail:

Travelling by trains gives one the advantage of witnessing the actual rustic India and enjoy the beauty. Maharashtra homes the highest-revenue earning railway station- Bhubaneswar Railway Station which is connected to various other Indian states. Also the major trains which ply on this route are Konark Express, Rajdhani and Coromandel Express make it rather very convenient for the travellers as the railway station is based in the capital city of Maharashtra..

Places to visit in Maharashtra/span>


Maharashtra- The Land of Unique Temples

1. Mumbai (Bombay) Review: Mumbai, the capital city of Maharashtra, is a bustling metropolis known for its vibrant culture, historic landmarks, and modern attractions. Key spots include the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, Colaba Causeway, Elephanta Caves, and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. The city's lively nightlife and Bollywood industry also make it a major draw. Highlights: Gateway of India, Marine Drive, Elephanta Caves, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, vibrant nightlife.
2. Pune Review: Pune, known as the "Oxford of the East," is a cultural and educational hub. It offers a blend of historical sites and modern attractions, including the Aga Khan Palace, Shaniwarwada Fort, and Osho Ashram. The city is also a gateway to the scenic hill stations of Matheran and Lonavala. Highlights: Aga Khan Palace, Shaniwarwada Fort, Osho Ashram, Sinhagad Fort, nearby hill stations.
3. Aurangabad Review: Aurangabad is renowned for its historical and architectural wonders. The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, both UNESCO World Heritage Sites, are major attractions. The city also features the Bibi Ka Maqbara, often referred to as the "Taj of the Deccan," and the Daulatabad Fort. Highlights: Ajanta Caves, Ellora Caves, Bibi Ka Maqbara, Daulatabad Fort.
4. Nashik Review: Nashik is known for its vineyards and wine industry, making it a popular destination for wine enthusiasts. The city also hosts the Kumbh Mela every 12 years. Key attractions include the Sula Vineyards, Panchavati Temple, and the Godavari River. Highlights: Sula Vineyards, Kumbh Mela, Panchavati Temple, Godavari River.
5. Lonavala and Khandala Review: Lonavala and Khandala are picturesque hill stations located in the Western Ghats. They are known for their lush greenery, pleasant climate, and scenic spots like the Bhushi Dam, Lonavala Lake, and Karla Caves. They are popular weekend getaways from Mumbai and Pune. Highlights: Bhushi Dam, Lonavala Lake, Karla Caves, scenic viewpoints.
6. Mahabaleshwar Review: Mahabaleshwar is a popular hill station known for its pleasant climate and panoramic views. Attractions include Venna Lake, Arthur's Seat, and the Mahabaleshwar Temple. The town is surrounded by lush greenery and offers several trekking opportunities. Highlights: Venna Lake, Arthur's Seat, Mahabaleshwar Temple, scenic views.
7. Ratnagiri Review: Ratnagiri is a coastal town known for its beaches, historical sites, and delicious mangoes. Attractions include the Ratnadurg Fort, Ganpatipule Beach, and the Thibaw Palace. The town offers a serene environment with beautiful coastal scenery. Highlights: Ratnadurg Fort, Ganpatipule Beach, Thibaw Palace, coastal beauty.
8. Sindhudurg Review: Sindhudurg is known for its coastal beauty and historical forts. The Sindhudurg Fort, built by Shivaji Maharaj, is a major attraction. The region also features beautiful beaches like Tarkarli and Malvan, as well as opportunities for water sports. Highlights: Sindhudurg Fort, Tarkarli Beach, Malvan, water sports.
9. Ajanta and Ellora Caves Review: These UNESCO World Heritage Sites are located near Aurangabad. The Ajanta Caves are renowned for their ancient Buddhist murals and paintings, while the Ellora Caves feature a remarkable blend of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain temples and monasteries. Highlights: Ajanta Caves murals, Ellora Caves temples, historical significance, architectural marvels.
10. Shirdi Review: Shirdi is a major pilgrimage site dedicated to Sai Baba. The Sai Baba Temple attracts millions of devotees each year. The town also has several other temples and sacred sites related to Sai Baba’s life. Highlights: Sai Baba Temple, pilgrimage site, spiritual experience.
11. Chikhaldara Review: Chikhaldara is a hill station in the Amravati district, known for its coffee plantations and scenic beauty. Attractions include the Melghat Tiger Reserve, the picturesque Bhim Kund, and the scenic viewpoints. Highlights: Melghat Tiger Reserve, Bhim Kund, coffee plantations, scenic viewpoints.
12. Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve Review: Located in the Chandrapur district, Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve is one of the oldest and largest national parks in Maharashtra. It offers excellent opportunities for tiger sightings and wildlife safaris. Highlights: Tiger safaris, rich wildlife, photography opportunities, biodiversity. Maharashtra offers a diverse range of experiences, from historical and architectural wonders to natural beauty and vibrant city life, making it a fascinating destination for travelers.
