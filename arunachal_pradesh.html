About ARUNACHAL-PRADESH


A true extraordinaire, Odisha is the quiet and serene part of India. Fringed by the Bay of Bengal, and flaunting a long coastline, this east Indian gem has some of the most stunning beaches that are often less-frequented. Home to a plethora of Hindu and Buddhist pilgrimage sites, Odisha is a place that will take you back to the glorious time of Ashoka the Great. Explore the majestic Jagannath Temple at Puri and learn about its many mysteries, view the medieval architecture of Odisha’s temple and Buddhist monasteries, attend the annual Sand Art Festival, go boating in the Chilika Lake, or walk on the sea at Chandipur Beach. There's so much to do in Odisha.


Odisha is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of Odisha is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, Odisha is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of Odisha. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in ARUNACHAL PRADESH


Summer in ARUNACHAL-PRADESH

Summer season in Odisha starts from April and lasts till June. During this, Odisha remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of Odisha for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in Odisha. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in ARUNACHAL-PRADESH

Monsoon season in Odisha begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of Odisha wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of Odisha, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of Odisha then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in ARUNACHAL-PRADESH

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around Odisha and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting Odisha during the winter season you would wiess the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to Odisha where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then Odisha will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

How to Reach ARUNACHAL-PRADESH


Odisha is that destination of India which gives you those memories which are hard to forget. This Indian state is well connected by air, rail and road to various other major cities of India, so reaching there would be hustle free. No matter in which state or for that matter, in which country you are in the world, reaching Odisha will never be difficult. To have a memorable experience one should choose the best alternative of travelling to Odisha and explore India in every manner be it from air or road or even while travelling through rail.


By Air:

Odisha has that one airport which is well connected to various nations of the world. Biju Patnaik International Airport is located in the capital city of Odisha and a world-based connectivity. Other than this, the Indian state has domestic as well as private airports. The state also has a domestic airport which is used for commercial purpose also, popularly known as Jharsuguda Airport. Airways is actually the best alternative to reach anywhere and reaching Odisha on time gives you rather more time to explore the gorgeous state.


By Road:

Reaching Odisha by road is quite a challenge yet an interesting one. The one who loves long drives surely will enjoy the national highways, long drives and beautiful roads. Also reaching Odisha is quite easy as the state has well road connectivity and access to various nearby cities. But for those who do not drive or don’t want to drive, the next best alternative is to board the buses which ply from various cities of India. These buses could be private or run by the government for establishing and maintaining a good connectivity with various states.


By Rail:

Travelling by trains gives one the advantage of witnessing the actual rustic India and enjoy the beauty. Odisha homes the highest-revenue earning railway station- Bhubaneswar Railway Station which is connected to various other Indian states. Also the major trains which ply on this route are Konark Express, Rajdhani and Coromandel Express make it rather very convenient for the travellers as the railway station is based in the capital city of Odisha..

Places to visit in ARUNACHAL-PRADESH


Odisha- The Land of Unique Temples

1.Tawang is famous for the Tawang Monastery, the largest monastery in India and an important center for Tibetan Buddhism. The picturesque town offers beautiful views of the Himalayas, serene lakes, and vibrant festivals. Highlights: Tawang Monastery, Sela Pass, Madhuri Lake, Nuranang Falls.
2. Ziro Valley Review: Ziro Valley is known for its lush green landscapes, rice fields, and the unique Apatani tribal culture. The Ziro Music Festival, held annually, attracts music lovers from all over the country. Highlights: Apatani tribal villages, Talley Valley Wildlife Sanctuary, Meghna Cave Temple.
3. Bomdila Review: Bomdila offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan ranges and is known for its Buddhist monasteries and apple orchards. The town is a gateway to the Tawang region and provides a serene atmosphere. Highlights: Bomdila Monastery, Eaglenest Wildlife Sanctuary, Bomdila View Point.
4. Itanagar Review: Itanagar, the capital city of Arunachal Pradesh, is known for its historical and cultural significance. The city houses the Ita Fort, Jawaharlal Nehru State Museum, and the Ganga Lake (Gyakar Sinyi). Highlights: Ita Fort, Ganga Lake, Jawaharlal Nehru State Museum, Itanagar Wildlife Sanctuary.
5. Pasighat Review: Pasighat, the oldest town in Arunachal Pradesh, is situated on the banks of the Siang River. It offers opportunities for adventure sports like river rafting and angling, along with stunning natural beauty. Highlights: Siang River, Daying Ering Wildlife Sanctuary, Pangin, Bodak Scenic Area.
6. Dirang Review: Dirang is a charming town known for its pleasant weather, apple orchards, and hot water springs. The town serves as a base for treks to the Dirang Dzong and Sangti Valley. Highlights: Dirang Dzong, Sangti Valley, Hot Water Springs, National Research Centre on Yak.
7. Roing Review: Roing is a beautiful town located in the Lower Dibang Valley and is known for its picturesque landscapes, ancient ruins, and vibrant tribal culture. The town is a great destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts. Highlights: Mayudia Pass, Bhismaknagar Fort, Mehao Wildlife Sanctuary, Sally Lake.
8. Along (Aalo) Review: Along, also known as Aalo, is a scenic town surrounded by mountains and rivers. The town is known for its beautiful hanging bridges and vibrant tribal culture. Highlights: Hanging Bridges, Patum Bridge, Donyi-Polo Temple, Kamki Hydropower Dam.
9. Bhalukpong Review: Bhalukpong, located on the border of Arunachal Pradesh and Assam, is known for its picturesque landscapes and adventure activities like river rafting and fishing. The town serves as the entry point to the Kameng region. Highlights: Kameng River, Tipi Orchid Research Centre, Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary.
10. Namdapha National Park Review: Namdapha National Park, located in the Changlang district, is the largest protected area in the Eastern Himalayas and is known for its rich biodiversity. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and the elusive snow leopard. Highlights: Wildlife spotting, bird watching, trekking, beautiful landscapes.
Arunachal Pradesh offers a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural experiences, making it a fascinating destination for travelers.

Things to do in ARUNACHAL-PRADESH


  • Try jet skiing, parasailing, or speed boating at the Puri Beach
  • Boat among the mangrove forests in the Bhitarkanika National Park and view the crocodiles at the Park
  • Visit the Kalijai Temple, Satapada Dolphin Sanctuary and boat around in Chilika Lake, the largest saltwater lake in India
  • Explore the Panch Pandav Caves and the Gupteshwar Caves
  • Visit the Badaghagara Waterfall, Duduma Waterfall, and the Khandadhar Waterfall
  • Check out Odisha's traditional handicrafts from the villages of Raghurajpur and Pipili
  • Check out the regional cuisine and the many sweets of Odisha
