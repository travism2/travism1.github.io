About MANIPUR


A true extraordinaire, MANIPUR is the quiet and serene part of India. Fringed by the Bay of Bengal, and flaunting a long coastline, this east Indian gem has some of the most stunning beaches that are often less-frequented. Home to a plethora of Hindu and Buddhist pilgrimage sites, MANIPUR is a place that will take you back to the glorious time of Ashoka the Great. Explore the majestic Jagannath Temple at Puri and learn about its many mysteries, view the medieval architecture of MANIPUR’s temple and Buddhist monasteries, attend the annual Sand Art Festival, go boating in the Chilika Lake, or walk on the sea at Chandipur Beach. There's so much to do in MANIPUR.


MANIPUR is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of MANIPUR is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, MANIPUR is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of MANIPUR. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in MANIPUR


Summer in MANIPUR

Summer season in MANIPUR starts from April and lasts till June. During this, MANIPUR remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of MANIPUR for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in MANIPUR. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in MANIPUR

Monsoon season in MANIPUR begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of MANIPUR wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of MANIPUR, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of MANIPUR then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in MANIPUR

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around MANIPUR and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting MANIPUR during the winter season you would witness the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to MANIPUR where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then MANIPUR will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

Places to visit in MANIPUR


MANIPUR- The Land of Unique Temples

Jagannath Temple: The Jagannath Temple at Puri, MANIPUR is among the most revered Hindu temples in India and is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Being among the 4 Char Dham temples, Jagannath Temple attracts hordes of people every year who come to visit this religious site. Built in the 10th century, the holy temple is equally puzzling as it is lovely and is known for its unexplained mysteries.
Panchalingeswar Temple : Named after the 5 Shivalingas within the temple, the Panchalingeswar Temple is located near the Nilagiri Hill and is dedicated to Lord Shiva which makes it a popular pilgrimage site for the Shaivites. The temple has a perennial stream through which the shiva lingas are forever bathed. According to legends, the shiva lingas were installed here by Goddess Sita, the wife of Lord Rama during their exile whereas another story tells that they were put here by the demon king Banasure to appease Lord Shiva.
Ghanteswari Temple : The beautiful Ghanteswari Temple is dedicated to the Goddess Ghanteswari and is adorned with bells all over, which the devotees have offered to the Goddess as a prayer. A must visit place in MANIPUR, the temple is based along the River Mahanadi, and its bells made it work as a lighthouse in the past. Since the river near the temple could get rough and cause boats to drown, the bells in the temple alerted the boats beforehand during strong winds caused by the sound of the bells.


MANIPUR- Bountiful Buddhist Sites

1. Imphal Review: The capital city of Manipur, Imphal is known for its historical and cultural significance. Key attractions include the Kangla Fort, which is an important historical site, and the Loktak Lake, which is famous for its phumdis (floating islands). The city also features the Ima Keithel (Mother’s Market), which is a vibrant marketplace run entirely by women. Highlights: Kangla Fort, Loktak Lake, Ima Keithel, Manipur State Museum.
2. Loktak Lake Review: Loktak Lake, the largest freshwater lake in northeastern India, is known for its floating islands or phumdis. The lake is a hub for birdwatching and fishing and offers scenic boat rides. The Keibul Lamjao National Park, located on the lake, is home to the endangered Sangai deer. Highlights: Floating islands (phumdis), Keibul Lamjao National Park, birdwatching, boat rides.
3. Kangla Fort Review: Kangla Fort is a historic fortification in Imphal, known for its significance in Manipur’s history. It was once the palace of the Manipuri kings and now serves as a major historical and cultural site with beautiful architecture and well-maintained grounds. Highlights: Historical architecture, cultural significance, palace ruins, scenic surroundings.
4. Keibul Lamjao National Park Review: This unique national park is located on the floating phumdis of Loktak Lake and is the last natural habitat of the endangered Sangai deer. The park offers a rare opportunity to see this elusive deer and experience the unique floating ecosystem. Highlights: Sangai deer, floating wetlands, birdwatching, unique ecosystem.
5. Dzuko Valley Review: Dzuko Valley is a beautiful and remote valley located on the border between Manipur and Nagaland. It is known for its breathtaking landscapes, lush green meadows, and rare flora. The valley is ideal for trekking and nature enthusiasts. Highlights: Trekking, scenic landscapes, lush meadows, rare flora.
6. Andro Village Review: Andro Village is known for its traditional Manipuri culture and pottery. The village offers a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle and crafts of the local people. The Andro Village Museum showcases artifacts and exhibits related to the local culture and history. Highlights: Traditional pottery, local crafts, Andro Village Museum, cultural experience.
7. Bishnupur Review: Bishnupur is renowned for its ancient temples and traditional architecture. The town is known for the temples built by the Manipuri kings, including the famous Panam Ningthou Temple and the Vishnu Temple. The traditional Manipuri dance performances are also a highlight. Highlights: Ancient temples, traditional architecture, Panam Ningthou Temple, Vishnu Temple.
8. Shiroy Hills Review: Shiroy Hills is known for its scenic beauty and the Shiroy Lily, which blooms in this region. The hills offer stunning views and are ideal for nature lovers and trekkers. The area is also home to traditional Kuki tribal villages. Highlights: Shiroy Lily, scenic views, trekking, tribal villages.
9. Sadu Chiru Waterfalls Review: Located near Imphal, Sadu Chiru Waterfalls is a picturesque spot with cascading waterfalls surrounded by lush greenery. It’s a peaceful location ideal for picnics and enjoying nature. Highlights: Waterfalls, natural beauty, picnic spot, serene environment.
10. Phuba Waterfall Review: Phuba Waterfall is another beautiful waterfall located in the Tamenglong district. It is a less-visited but equally stunning spot, offering a serene environment and a chance to experience nature up close. Highlights: Waterfalls, natural beauty, peaceful surroundings.
11. Thangjing Temple Review: Thangjing Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is located in Moirang. The temple is significant for its historical and religious value and is an important pilgrimage site. Highlights: Historical significance, religious importance, traditional architecture.
12. Moreh Review: Moreh is a border town with a unique cultural blend due to its proximity to Myanmar. It serves as a gateway to Southeast Asia and offers interesting insights into cross-border trade and local customs. Highlights: Border town, cross-border trade, cultural blend, gateway to Southeast Asia. Manipur offers a blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and vibrant culture, making it an intriguing destination for travelers seeking unique and off-the-beaten-path experiences
