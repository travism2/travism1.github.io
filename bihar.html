About BIHAR


A true extraordinaire, BIHAR is the quiet and serene part of India. Fringed by the Bay of Bengal, and flaunting a long coastline, this east Indian gem has some of the most stunning beaches that are often less-frequented. Home to a plethora of Hindu and Buddhist pilgrimage sites, BIHAR is a place that will take you back to the glorious time of Ashoka the Great. Explore the majestic Jagannath Temple at Puri and learn about its many mysteries, view the medieval architecture of BIHAR’s temple and Buddhist monasteries, attend the annual Sand Art Festival, go boating in the Chilika Lake, or walk on the sea at Chandipur Beach. There's so much to do in BIHAR.


BIHAR is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of BIHAR is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, BIHAR is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of BIHAR. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in BIHAR


Summer in BIHAR

Summer season in BIHAR starts from April and lasts till June. During this, BIHAR remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of BIHAR for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in BIHAR. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in BIHAR

Monsoon season in BIHAR begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of BIHAR wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of BIHAR, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of BIHAR then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in BIHAR

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around BIHAR and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting BIHAR during the winter season you would witness the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to BIHAR where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then BIHAR will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

Places to visit in BIHAR


BIHAR- The Land of Unique Temples

1. Bodh Gaya Review: Bodh Gaya is one of the most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world, as it is where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree. The Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the central attraction, surrounded by monasteries and meditation centers from various Buddhist countries. Highlights: Mahabodhi Temple, Bodhi Tree, Great Buddha Statue, Thai Monastery.
2. Nalanda Review: Nalanda is renowned for the ruins of Nalanda University, one of the world's oldest universities and a significant center of learning in ancient times. The Nalanda Archaeological Museum houses artifacts recovered from the site. Highlights: Nalanda University ruins, Nalanda Archaeological Museum, Hiuen Tsang Memorial Hall.
3. Rajgir Review: Rajgir is a historic town with connections to both Buddhism and Jainism. It features the Griddhakuta Hill, where Buddha delivered several sermons, and the Vishwa Shanti Stupa, a peace pagoda. The hot springs and ancient cyclopean walls are also notable attractions. Highlights: Griddhakuta Hill, Vishwa Shanti Stupa, Hot Springs, Cyclopean Walls.
4. Patna Review: Patna, the capital city of Bihar, has a rich history dating back to ancient times. Key attractions include the Patna Sahib Gurudwara, one of the holiest Sikh shrines, and the Patna Museum, which houses a collection of artifacts from the region. Highlights: Patna Sahib Gurudwara, Patna Museum, Golghar, Gandhi Maidan.
5. Vaishali Review: Vaishali is an ancient city with historical and religious significance. It is believed to be the place where Lord Buddha preached his last sermon. The Ashoka Pillar, a relic of Emperor Ashoka, and the ruins of ancient stupas are key attractions. Highlights: Ashoka Pillar, Vishwa Shanti Stupa, Archaeological Museum.
6. Pawapuri Review: Pawapuri is an important pilgrimage site for Jains, as it is the place where Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara, attained Nirvana. The Jal Mandir, a temple in the middle of a lotus pond, is a major attraction. Highlights: Jal Mandir, Samosharan, Pawapuri Museum.
7. Bhagalpur Review: Bhagalpur is known for its silk industry and historical sites. The Vikramshila University ruins, an ancient center of Buddhist learning, are a key attraction. The nearby Kuppaghat Ashram is also notable. Highlights: Vikramshila University ruins, Kuppaghat Ashram, Mandar Hill.
8. Gaya Review: Gaya is another significant pilgrimage site, especially for Hindus. The Vishnupad Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is a major attraction. Gaya is also known for the annual Pind Daan rituals performed by Hindus for their ancestors. Highlights: Vishnupad Temple, Pind Daan rituals, Dungeshwari Cave Temples.
9. Sasaram Review: Sasaram is known for the grand tomb of Sher Shah Suri, an impressive example of Indo-Islamic architecture. The town also features the Rohtasgarh Fort, a historical fort with scenic views. Highlights: Sher Shah Suri Tomb, Rohtasgarh Fort, Maa Tara Chandi Temple.
10. Kesaria Review: Kesaria is home to the Kesaria Stupa, one of the tallest and largest Buddhist stupas in the world. It is believed to have been built to commemorate the place where Buddha spent his last days before attaining Parinirvana. Highlights: Kesaria Stupa, nearby archaeological sites. Bihar offers a rich tapestry of cultural, historical, and religious experiences, making it a fascinating destination for travelers interested in exploring India's ancient heritage.

Things to do in BIHAR

  • Boat among the mangrove forests in the Bhitarkanika National Park and view the crocodiles at the Park
  • Visit the Kalijai Temple, Satapada Dolphin Sanctuary and boat around in Chilika Lake, the largest saltwater lake in India
  • Explore the Panch Pandav Caves and the Gupteshwar Caves
  • Visit the Badaghagara Waterfall, Duduma Waterfall, and the Khandadhar Waterfall
  • Check out BIHAR's traditional handicrafts from the villages of Raghurajpur and Pipili
  • Check out the regional cuisine and the many sweets of BIHAR
