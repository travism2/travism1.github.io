About GOA


Goa, located on the western coast of India, is famous for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, rich Portuguese heritage, and diverse cultural experiences.


GOA is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of GOA is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, GOA is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of GOA. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in GOA


Summer in GOA

Summer season in GOA starts from April and lasts till June. During this, GOA remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of GOA for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in GOA. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in GOA

Monsoon season in GOA begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of GOA wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of GOA, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of GOA then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in GOA

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around GOA and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting GOA during the winter season you would witness the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to GOA where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then GOA will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

How to Reach GOA


GOA is that destination of India which gives you those memories which are hard to forget. This Indian state is well connected by air, rail and road to various other major cities of India, so reaching there would be hustle free. No matter in which state or for that matter, in which country you are in the world, reaching GOA will never be difficult. To have a memorable experience one should choose the best alternative of travelling to GOA and explore India in every manner be it from air or road or even while travelling through rail.


By Air:

GOA has that one airport which is well connected to various nations of the world. Biju Patnaik International Airport is located in the capital city of GOA and a world-based connectivity. Other than this, the Indian state has domestic as well as private airports. The state also has a domestic airport which is used for commercial purpose also, popularly known as Jharsuguda Airport. Airways is actually the best alternative to reach anywhere and reaching GOA on time gives you rather more time to explore the gorgeous state.


By Road:

Reaching GOA by road is quite a challenge yet an interesting one. The one who loves long drives surely will enjoy the national highways, long drives and beautiful roads. Also reaching GOA is quite easy as the state has well road connectivity and access to various nearby cities. But for those who do not drive or don’t want to drive, the next best alternative is to board the buses which ply from various cities of India. These buses could be private or run by the government for establishing and maintaining a good connectivity with various states.


By Rail:

Travelling by trains gives one the advantage of witnessing the actual rustic India and enjoy the beauty. GOA homes the highest-revenue earning railway station- Bhubaneswar Railway Station which is connected to various other Indian states. Also the major trains which ply on this route are Konark Express, Rajdhani and Coromandel Express make it rather very convenient for the travellers as the railway station is based in the capital city of GOA..

Places to visit in GOA


GOA- The Western Coast

1. Calangute Beach Review: Known as the "Queen of Beaches," Calangute Beach is one of the most popular and largest beaches in North Goa. It attracts a large number of tourists and offers a lively atmosphere with water sports, beach shacks, and vibrant nightlife. Highlights: Water sports, beach shacks, nightlife, shopping. 2. Baga Beach Review: Adjacent to Calangute Beach, Baga Beach is famous for its vibrant nightlife, including popular clubs and bars like Tito's and Mambo's. It also offers a range of water sports and beach activities. Highlights: Nightlife, water sports, beach parties, dining options. 3. Anjuna Beach Review: Anjuna Beach is renowned for its scenic beauty, rocky formations, and the famous Anjuna Flea Market held every Wednesday. It's a favorite spot for backpackers and those seeking a laid-back vibe. Highlights: Flea market, beach parties, scenic views, casual dining. 4. Vagator Beach Review: Known for its dramatic cliffs and vibrant atmosphere, Vagator Beach is a beautiful spot with both a lively and a serene side. The Chapora Fort, overlooking the beach, offers stunning views of the coastline. Highlights: Chapora Fort, sunset views, beach parties, picturesque landscape. 5. Palolem Beach Review: Located in South Goa, Palolem Beach is famous for its crescent shape and palm-fringed shores. It is relatively quieter than the northern beaches, making it ideal for relaxation and tranquility. Highlights: Scenic beauty, relaxed atmosphere, kayaking, dolphin spotting. 6. Dudhsagar Falls Review: Dudhsagar Falls, located on the Goa-Karnataka border, is one of India's tallest waterfalls. The cascading water resembles a sea of milk, offering a spectacular sight, especially during the monsoon season. Highlights: Trekking, jeep safari, natural beauty, photography. 7. Old Goa Review: Old Goa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is known for its impressive colonial architecture and historic churches. Key attractions include the Basilica of Bom Jesus, Sé Cathedral, and Church of St. Francis of Assisi. Highlights: Historic churches, colonial architecture, museums, religious significance. 8. Panaji (Panjim) Review: The capital city of Goa, Panaji, is known for its charming streets, vibrant markets, and beautiful riverfront. The Latin Quarter, with its colorful Portuguese-style houses, and the Church of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception are major attractions. Highlights: Latin Quarter, river cruises, historic churches, local markets. 9. Aguada Fort Review: Built by the Portuguese in the 17th century, Aguada Fort is a well-preserved fort offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea. The fort also houses a lighthouse and is a popular tourist spot. Highlights: Historical significance, panoramic views, lighthouse. 10. Arambol Beach Review: Arambol Beach is a quieter, more laid-back beach known for its bohemian vibe and scenic beauty. It attracts a mix of tourists, including artists and backpackers, and offers activities like paragliding and yoga. Highlights: Bohemian atmosphere, scenic beauty, paragliding, yoga retreats. 11. Colva Beach Review: One of the longest beaches in South Goa, Colva Beach is known for its white sand, swaying palm trees, and vibrant nightlife. It offers a variety of water sports and beach shacks serving delicious seafood. Highlights: Long stretches of white sand, water sports, nightlife, seafood. 12. Butterfly Beach Review: Butterfly Beach is a secluded and picturesque beach known for its beautiful landscape and tranquility. It is accessible by boat or through a forest hike and offers a serene escape from the more crowded beaches. Highlights: Secluded atmosphere, scenic views, boat rides, hiking. Goa offers a diverse range of experiences, from bustling beaches and vibrant nightlife to tranquil landscapes and rich historical sites, making it a popular destination for all types of travelers

Things to do in GOA


  • Try jet skiing, parasailing, or speed boating at the Puri Beach
  • Boat among the mangrove forests in the Bhitarkanika National Park and view the crocodiles at the Park
  • Visit the Kalijai Temple, Satapada Dolphin Sanctuary and boat around in Chilika Lake, the largest saltwater lake in India
  • Explore the Panch Pandav Caves and the Gupteshwar Caves
  • Visit the Badaghagara Waterfall, Duduma Waterfall, and the Khandadhar Waterfall
  • Check out GOA's traditional handicrafts from the villages of Raghurajpur and Pipili
  • Check out the regional cuisine and the many sweets of GOA
