About GUJARAT


Gujarat, located on the western coast of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and diverse landscapes. Here are some of the main tourist spots in Gujarat


GUJARAT is blessed with a varied landscape and consists of hills, rivers, beaches and sea, making it a popular point of interest for the tourists. Its forests make the perfect home for a number of wildlife such as Tigers, Elephants, Black Panthers, Macaque, Bear, Gharial, Chital and the Sambar Deer. The architecture of GUJARAT is another reason to fall in love with the state with several historic structures adorned with intricate carvings like the famed Sun Temple of Konark, which became the inspiration for the Stone Chariot at Hampi. Not just rich in history, GUJARAT is also rich with minerals. An important hub for India, the state has reserves of Iron ore, Bauxite, Chromite, Manganese ore, Coal, Limestone, Graphite, Gold, Gemstone, Diamond, among others. It is indeed true that words cannot express the beauty of GUJARAT. Home to one of the four sacred Hindu pilgrimages, the state will satiate the curiosity of a traveler who is curious to learn about the elements that paint its canvas.

Best Time to Visit in GUJARAT


Summer in GUJARAT

Summer season in GUJARAT starts from April and lasts till June. During this, GUJARAT remains hot and humid with temperature lingering between 24°C - 35°C. However, if you are up to plan your trip during the summer season to experience the scorching heat of GUJARAT for a unique experience then you can go ahead as the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer season in GUJARAT. In fact, summer is a good time to enjoy and celebrate festivals like Chandan Yatra, Maha Vishuva Sankranti, Raja Praba and Rath Yatra


Monsoon in GUJARAT

Monsoon season in GUJARAT begins from the month of July and lasts till September. People of GUJARAT wait for the arrival of the monsoon as the state expect heavy rains lasting till September which enhances the beauty of GUJARAT, making it more green and alluring. If you are up to experience the incessant rain of GUJARAT then go ahead planning a trip by seeking travel advice that would help you cover everything from the scratch.


Winter in GUJARAT

Winters between October and February is the most suitable to get around GUJARAT and explore the beautiful state. The weather is cool, pleasant and perfect with 10°C. Visiting GUJARAT during the winter season you would witness the lush greenery all around and will have the opportunity to reminisce the scintillating views. The pleasant weather of the season would allow you to make the most out of your trip to GUJARAT where you can roam around the temples easily. If you are a nature lover, then GUJARAT will provide you with a lifetime experience where you can witness the migratory birds.

How to Reach GUJARAT


GUJARAT is that destination of India which gives you those memories which are hard to forget. This Indian state is well connected by air, rail and road to various other major cities of India, so reaching there would be hustle free. No matter in which state or for that matter, in which country you are in the world, reaching GUJARAT will never be difficult. To have a memorable experience one should choose the best alternative of travelling to GUJARAT and explore India in every manner be it from air or road or even while travelling through rail.


By Air:

GUJARAT has that one airport which is well connected to various nations of the world. Biju Patnaik International Airport is located in the capital city of GUJARAT and a world-based connectivity. Other than this, the Indian state has domestic as well as private airports. The state also has a domestic airport which is used for commercial purpose also, popularly known as Jharsuguda Airport. Airways is actually the best alternative to reach anywhere and reaching GUJARAT on time gives you rather more time to explore the gorgeous state.


By Road:

Reaching GUJARAT by road is quite a challenge yet an interesting one. The one who loves long drives surely will enjoy the national highways, long drives and beautiful roads. By Rail:

Places to visit in GUJARAT


GUJARAT- The Land of Unique Temples

1. Ahmedabad Review: Ahmedabad, the largest city in Gujarat, is a bustling metropolis known for its blend of historical and modern attractions. The Sabarmati Ashram, associated with Mahatma Gandhi, is a must-visit. The city's architectural wonders include the Adalaj Stepwell and the Jama Masjid. Highlights: Sabarmati Ashram, Adalaj Stepwell, Jama Masjid, Kankaria Lake, vibrant markets.
2. Rann of Kutch Review: The Great Rann of Kutch is a vast salt desert that transforms into a white wonderland during the Rann Utsav, a cultural festival held annually. The festival showcases local crafts, folk music, and dance, offering a unique cultural experience. Highlights: Rann Utsav, salt desert, cultural performances, handicrafts.
3. Gir National Park Review: Gir National Park is the only place in the world where you can see Asiatic lions in their natural habitat. The park also houses a variety of other wildlife, including leopards, deer, and numerous bird species. Highlights: Asiatic lions, wildlife safaris, bird watching, rich biodiversity.
4. Somnath Temple Review: The Somnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva and holds immense religious significance. Located on the western coast, the temple offers stunning sea views and an aura of spirituality. Highlights: Religious significance, sea views, stunning architecture, spiritual atmosphere.
5. Dwarka Review: Dwarka is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is believed to be the ancient kingdom of Lord Krishna. The Dwarkadhish Temple is the main attraction, drawing devotees and tourists alike. Highlights: Dwarkadhish Temple, religious significance, coastal views, ancient history.
6. Statue of Unity Review: The Statue of Unity, dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, is the world's tallest statue. Located near the Sardar Sarovar Dam, the statue offers breathtaking views and is surrounded by attractions like the Valley of Flowers and the Sardar Patel Museum. Highlights: World's tallest statue, panoramic views, nearby attractions, historical significance.
7. Saputara Review: Saputara is a picturesque hill station in the Sahyadri range, known for its pleasant climate and scenic beauty. It offers attractions like the Saputara Lake, Sunset Point, and the artist village. Highlights: Scenic beauty, boating on Saputara Lake, Sunset Point, trekking, cultural experiences.
8. Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park Review: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park is known for its well-preserved Islamic and Hindu architecture. Key attractions include the Jama Masjid, Kalika Mata Temple, and numerous historical structures. Highlights: Historical significance, UNESCO site, architectural marvels, panoramic views.
9. Rani ki Vav Review: Rani ki Vav, located in Patan, is an intricately designed stepwell and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 11th century, it showcases stunning sculptures and architectural brilliance. Highlights: Architectural beauty, historical significance, detailed carvings, UNESCO site.
10. Bhuj Review: Bhuj is known for its rich history and cultural heritage. Key attractions include the Aina Mahal (Mirror Palace), Prag Mahal, and the Bhujia Fort. The city is also a gateway to the Rann of Kutch. Highlights: Historical sites, palaces, handicrafts, gateway to Rann of Kutch.
11. Junagadh Review: Junagadh is a city steeped in history, with attractions like the Uparkot Fort, Mahabat Maqbara, and the Girnar Hills. The Girnar Hills are also a significant pilgrimage site with a series of Jain temples. Highlights: Uparkot Fort, Mahabat Maqbara, Girnar Hills, historical and religious significance.
12. Porbandar Review: Porbandar is the birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi and offers attractions related to his life, including the Kirti Mandir, his ancestral home. The city also has beautiful coastal views. Highlights: Kirti Mandir, coastal views, Sudama Temple, historical significance.
13. Lothal Review: Lothal is an ancient city of the Indus Valley Civilization and is one of the most important archaeological sites in India. The site offers insights into the advanced urban planning and maritime trade of the ancient civilization. Highlights: Archaeological site, Indus Valley Civilization, historical significance, museum.
Gujarat offers a diverse array of attractions, from natural wonders and wildlife to rich cultural and historical sites, making it a fascinating destination for travelers.
